%% ── Three Claims ──
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!7,colframe=gray!55,boxrule=0.4pt,arc=2pt,left=4pt,right=4pt,top=2pt,bottom=2pt]
{\small
\textbf{Claim 1: Number-theoretic necessity of a 5-dimensional background space}\enspace
By Lagrange's four-square theorem and projective-geometric conditions, full-sphere coverage in discrete space requires exactly 5 dimensions. Our ``3 spatial $+$ 1 temporal'' dimensions follow from number theory.{\scriptsize\ (W5)}\\[2pt]
\textbf{Claim 2: Combinatorial derivation of 62 Standard Model states}\enspace
From a 6-dimensional structure---a 5D hypercube plus an 8-valued discrete label axis $Q$---62 states are derived by pure enumeration, without any physics terminology. These correspond to the 61 SM particles $+$ the graviton.{\scriptsize\ (W8)}\\[2pt]
\textbf{Claim 3: Unified description of the four forces}\enspace
The 6D axes are classified into position-type $\{x,y,z,t\}$, scale-type $\{R\}$, and color-type $\{Q\}$. Electromagnetism, the weak force, the strong force, and gravity are all described as the same operation---``axial displacement transfer''---applied to different axis types.{\scriptsize\ (W10, W11)}}
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{multicols}{2}
\small

\textbf{I. Foundation Series (Papers 1--10)}\enspace
From the geometric properties of central projection alone: equivalence with de~Sitter spacetime, four symmetries, nested structure, and the SdS exact solution. Paper 8 Rev.2 formalizes dense packing of unit cubes into a 4D ball; via packing density $\rho(k) \to 1$ and the Lagrange--Jacobi four-square structure, $n=4$ is shown to be the minimal stable dimension, and the 1-vs-3 asymmetry gives rise to the time axis.

\smallskip
\textbf{II. Phase Equation Series (W1--W11 + S1\,\textperiodcentered\,S2 + 6 supplements)}\enspace
Introduces the combinatorial structure of hypercubes. W5 proves the necessity of 5 dimensions; W6--7 derive spin classification; \textbf{W8 derives 62 states}; W10--11 classify shape-invariant wave modes and unify the four forces. Supplements address CKM/PMNS matrices, the Higgs mechanism, and mass structure.

\smallskip
\textbf{III. Integration Papers (M1--M3)}\enspace
Bridge between the Foundation Series (continuous geometry) and the Phase Equation Series (discrete combinatorics). Formalize the nested structure via three curvature radii $R, R_0, R_1$.

\smallskip
\textbf{IV. Delay Circuit Series (5 papers)}\enspace
Independent topic. Constructs oscillation, propagation, collision, and wave-packet collapse from ``delayed information transfer'' alone.

\textbf{What this research does \emph{not} claim}

\vspace{1pt}
\begin{itemize}[topsep=1pt]
\item A physical ``derivation'' of the Standard Model
\item Numerical values of masses, coupling constants, or mixing matrices
\item Prediction of undiscovered particles
\item Rigorous reconstruction of gauge groups in the continuum limit
\end{itemize}
