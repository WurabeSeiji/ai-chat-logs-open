## Three Claims

**Claim 1: Number-theoretic necessity of a 5-dimensional background space**  
By Lagrange's four-square theorem and projective-geometric conditions, full-sphere coverage in discrete space requires exactly 5 dimensions. Our "3 spatial + 1 temporal" dimensions follow from number theory. (W5)

**Claim 2: Combinatorial derivation of 62 Standard Model states**  
From a 6-dimensional structure — a 5D hypercube plus an 8-valued discrete label axis Q — 62 states are derived by pure enumeration, without any physics terminology. These correspond to the 61 SM particles + the graviton. (W8)

**Claim 3: Unified description of the four forces**  
The 6D axes are classified into position-type {x,y,z,t}, scale-type {R}, and color-type {Q}. Electromagnetism, the weak force, the strong force, and gravity are all described as the same operation — "axial displacement transfer" — applied to different axis types. (W10, W11)

## Research Structure

**I. Foundation Series (Papers 1–10)**  
From the geometric properties of central projection alone: equivalence with de Sitter spacetime, four symmetries, nested structure, and the SdS exact solution. Derives that n=4 is the minimal stable dimension and that the 1-vs-3 asymmetry gives rise to the time axis.

**II. Phase Equation Series (W1–W11 + S1·S2 + 6 supplements)**  
Introduces the combinatorial structure of hypercubes. W5 proves the necessity of 5 dimensions; W6–7 derive spin classification; **W8 derives 62 states**; W10–11 classify shape-invariant wave modes and unify the four forces. Supplements address CKM/PMNS matrices, the Higgs mechanism, and mass structure.

**III. Integration Papers (M1–M3)**  
Bridge between the Foundation Series (continuous geometry) and the Phase Equation Series (discrete combinatorics). Formalize the nested structure via three curvature radii R, R₀, R₁.

**IV. Delay Circuit Series (5 papers)**  
Independent topic. Constructs oscillation, propagation, collision, and wave-packet collapse from "delayed information transfer" alone.

## What This Research Does *Not* Claim

- A physical "derivation" of the Standard Model
- Numerical values of masses, coupling constants, or mixing matrices
- Prediction of undiscovered particles
- Rigorous reconstruction of gauge groups in the continuum limit

The correspondence with the SM in W8 §9 is "one possible interpretation," not a claim of the paper.
