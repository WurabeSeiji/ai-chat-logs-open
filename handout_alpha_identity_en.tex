{\small
\textbf{Main Result: a self-consistent algebraic identity predicts $\alpha$ to 0.02\%}\enspace
Two elementary 4D geometric quantities --- $N(1) = 137$ (the count of integer-centred unit cubes fully contained in a 4D ball of radius 3, by direct enumeration) and $V_4(1) = \pi^2/2$ (the 4D unit ball volume by standard formula) --- satisfy the self-consistent equation
\[ \alpha^{-1} = N(1) + V_4(1)\,\alpha = 137 + \tfrac{\pi^2}{2}\alpha \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \tfrac{\pi^2}{2}\alpha^2 + 137\alpha - 1 = 0 \]
whose positive root $\alpha = 0.0072984$ matches the CODATA 2018 observed value $0.0072974$ with relative error $1.4\times 10^{-4}$ (\textbf{0.02\%}).\\[2pt]
\textbf{Decisive differences from the Eddington trap}\enspace
(1) Not pure integer-fitting; a \textbf{self-consistent equation containing $\alpha$ itself}. (2) Both the integer 137 and the correction coefficient $\pi^2/2$ are \textbf{derived directly and independently from 4D geometry} (not borrowed). (3) The perturbation expansion $1 = 137\alpha + V_4(1)\alpha^2$ is structurally analogous to QFT renormalisation. (4) Candidate physical mechanisms are proposed from independent thought-experiment work.\\[2pt]
\textbf{Caveat}\enspace
This paper does not claim a first-principles derivation of $\alpha$. What is presented is an observed numerical structure. Whether that reflects geometric necessity or numerical coincidence is left as an open problem.}
\end{tcolorbox}

\textbf{$N(1) = 137$: direct enumeration}\enspace
The condition $\sum_i (|c_i|+1/2)^2 \le 9$ for an integer-centred unit cube to be fully contained in the 4D ball of radius $R=3$. Enumerating by absolute-value patterns $(s_1,\ldots,s_4)$:

\smallskip
$(2,1,1,0)$ and beyond fail $\sum s_i^2 + \sum s_i \le 8$. Fully verifiable by hand.

\smallskip
\textbf{$V_4(1) = \pi^2/2$: standard formula}\enspace
$V_n(R) = \pi^{n/2}/\Gamma(n/2+1) \cdot R^n$ at $n=4, R=1$: $V_4(1) = \pi^2/\Gamma(3) = \pi^2/2 \approx 4.9348$. Coincides with the Planck-unit 4+1D BH entropy $S_{BH}(1) = \pi^2/2$ (cross-reference).

\smallskip
\textbf{Perturbative structure}\enspace
The equivalent form $1 = 137\alpha + (\pi^2/2)\alpha^2$ is a power expansion in $\alpha$: tree-level $137\alpha$ + first-order correction $V_4(1)\alpha^2$ summing to unity. Analogous to QFT renormalisation.

\smallskip
\textbf{Inside / outside decomposition}\enspace
The R=3 ball has volume $V_4(3) = 81\pi^2/2 \approx 399.72$. Of this, 137 cubes occupy the interior (34\%); the boundary gap is \textbf{66\%}. Boundary-dominated.
\begin{itemize}[topsep=1pt]
\item $137\alpha$: inside, 137 geometric contact points
\item $V_4(1)\alpha^2$: outside, boundary self-energy correction
\end{itemize}
