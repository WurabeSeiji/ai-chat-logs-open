## Five observations

A summary of what was recorded chronologically.

### Observation 1: Two meanings of R

In the 6-dimensional sign vector xyztRQ of the central projection framework, R carries two distinct meanings.

- The 5th component R of the sign vector: a particle classification label (±1, 0, etc.)
- The actual local curvature radius R': a physical quantity computed as c²/|acceleration|

These have often been conflated, but a vector component and a scalar are different.

### Observation 2: The Q axis also contributes to R'

If all 6 axes of xyztRQ are structurally symmetric, there is no reason that only Q should not contribute to R'. Q is a discrete internal symmetry label (color charge, etc.), but it may also affect the local geometric scale.

### Observation 3: δt is fundamental, t is just a sign component

The t (sign vector component) and δt (processing parameter) are distinct.

- t: the 4th component of the sign vector (particle classification label)
- δt: the processing step driving the evolution of all axes

With this distinction, 6-axis symmetry holds at the level of "contribution to phase change per δt".

The standard definition of acceleration (time derivative) does not apply here. The sign vector of a particle is an invariant fixed at creation, and acceleration emerges as a discrete phenomenon — interaction events (particle annihilation and new particle creation).

### Observation 4: xyztRQ are all phases

xyztR are continuous phases (U(1)), and Q is a discrete phase (Z_n). All 6 axes can be unified as phases.

Phase has three properties simultaneously:

- Continuous (representable as real numbers)
- Bounded (wrapping at mod 360 degrees, etc.)
- Discretely representable (arbitrary precision in int256)

This three-way coexistence is unique to phase. Phase is the only mathematical structure that bridges continuous and discrete.

### Observation 5: Implications of a 6D Wilson extension

Putting these together, the framework can potentially be read as a 6-dimensional extension of standard gauge theory.

- 4D xyzt: Wilson lattice gauge theory (structural correspondence already shown in Paper 8)
- R axis: central projection background, cosmological curvature (connected to general relativity)
- Q axis: internal symmetries (Standard Model)

However, this is observation, not formal proof. Complete formalization is left to subsequent research.
